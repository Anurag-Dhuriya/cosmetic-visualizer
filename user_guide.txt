## What This App Does

The Cosmetic Visualizer lets you upload a patient photo and simulate how different cosmetic treatments (fillers, lifts, contouring) would look — before any actual procedure is done.

It is designed for **visualization and consultation purposes only**.

---

## Before You Start

Make sure both the backend and frontend servers are running.

**Terminal 1 — Start the backend:**
```bash
cd cosmetic-visualizer
source venv/bin/activate        # Mac/Linux
# OR: venv\Scripts\activate     # Windows
uvicorn app.main:app --reload --port 8000
```

**Terminal 2 — Start the frontend:**
```bash
cd cosmetic-visualizer
npm run dev
```

Then open your browser and go to:
```
http://localhost:5173
```

## Using the App Step by Step

### Step 1 — Upload a Photo

- Click the **"Upload Photo"** button in the top-right of the preview area
- Select a photo from your device
- ✅ Accepted formats: **JPG, JPEG, PNG**
- ✅ Maximum file size: **10 MB**
- The photo will appear in the preview panel once uploaded

> **Best results:** Use a clear, front-facing photo with good lighting and a neutral expression. Avoid sunglasses, heavy shadows, or extreme angles.

---

### Step 2 — Pick a Category

In the **left sidebar**, click one of the four categories:

| Category | What it affects |
|----------|----------------|
| **Face** | Temples, cheeks, chin, jawline, forehead, folds |
| **Lips** | Overall volume, shape, corners |
| **Nose** | Bridge, tip, width, root |
| **Eyebrows** | Height and arch |

The selected category will highlight in blue.

---

### Step 3 — Pick a Treatment

In the **right panel**, the **Treatment** dropdown will show all available treatments for your selected category.

Click the dropdown and select the specific treatment you want to simulate.

---

### Step 4 — Adjust the Intensity

Use the **Intensity slider** (0% to 100%) to control how strong the effect is.

- **Low (0–30%)** — subtle, natural-looking result
- **Medium (40–70%)** — noticeable but realistic
- **High (80–100%)** — strong, exaggerated effect (useful for testing)

---

### Step 5 — Adjust Position (Optional)

Use the **X** and **Y position sliders** to fine-tune where the effect is applied:

- **X slider** — moves the effect left or right
- **Y slider** — moves the effect up or down

Leave both at **0** to use the automatically detected position from the face landmarks.

---

### Step 6 — Click Preview (Fast)

Click the **"Preview (fast)"** button to see the result.

- This runs the transformation on a **lower resolution copy** of the image
- It is fast (usually 1–3 seconds)
- A spinning loader will appear while it's processing
- The result will appear in the preview panel

> Use this for quick back-and-forth adjustments.

---

### Step 7 — Apply Final (Full Resolution)

When you are happy with the preview, click **"Apply Final"**.

- This runs the transformation on the **full resolution original image**
- It takes longer (5–15 seconds depending on image size)
- The high-quality result will appear in the preview panel

---

### Step 8 — Dark / Light Mode

Click the **moon / sun icon** in the top-right header to toggle between dark and light mode.

## Tips for Best Results

- **Use a well-lit, front-facing photo** — side angles or tilted heads reduce accuracy
- **Neutral expression works best** — smiling or frowning affects landmark detection
- **Plain background** helps the face detector focus on the face
- **Start with low intensity** (20–40%) and increase gradually
- **Try Preview (fast) first** to find the right settings, then click Apply Final for the quality result
- **One treatment at a time** — apply and review each treatment individually
- If the result looks off, try adjusting the X/Y position sliders slightly

---

## Known Limitations

- Only **one face per photo** is supported
- Works best on **front-facing photos** — profile or extreme angles may not detect landmarks correctly
- **Accessories** like glasses, masks, or heavy makeup may affect accuracy
- The app is a **visualization tool only** — results do not represent guaranteed outcomes of real procedures
- Very small or low-resolution images (under 200×200px) may not detect faces reliably

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Photo uploads but nothing shows | Backend not running | Check Terminal 1 shows `Uvicorn running` |
| "No face detected" error | Photo angle or quality | Use a clearer, front-facing photo |
| Spinner keeps spinning | Backend crashed | Check Terminal 1 for error messages |
| App won't open in browser | Frontend not running | Check Terminal 2 and run `npm run dev` |
| Result looks identical to original | Intensity too low | Increase intensity slider to 50%+ |
| "Could not connect to backend" | Wrong port or backend stopped | Restart backend with `uvicorn app.main:app --reload --port 8000` |
| Using on another device and can't connect | Using `localhost` in `API_BASE` | Update `API_BASE` in `App.tsx` to your computer's local IP |
